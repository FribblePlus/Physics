Newton's Laws of motion

What is mass?

  • Mass is the measure of the amount of matter in an object
  • Consequently, this is the property of an object that resists change in motion
  • The SI unit for mass is the kilogram (kg)

What is weight?

  • Weight is the effect of a gravitational field on a mass
  • It is measured in Newtons and is a vector quantity
  • W = mg

Mass vs Weight:

  • Mass is always constant, while weight may vary depending on the strength of gravitational field
  • For example, the gravitational field strength on the Moon is 1.63 N kg-1, meaning an object’s weight will be about 6 times less than on Earth

What is free fall?

  • An object is free fall if it is falling only due to gravity
  • On free fall equals to 9.81m/s^2

Resultant force:

  • Sum of all vector forces acting on a body

Force

  • Force is the rate of change of momentum
  • F=ma

Newton’s laws of motion:

  • Newton’s First Law: A body will remain at rest or move with constant velocity unless acted on by a resultant force
  • Newton’s Second Law: A resultant force acting on a body will cause a change in momentum in the direction of the force. The rate of change in momentum is proportional to the magnitude of the force (aka F = ma)
  • Newton’s Third Law: If an object A exerts a force on object B, then object B must exert a force of equal magnitude and opposite direction back on object A.

Linear Momentum

  • Product of mass and velocity. Momentum = m * v

Drag force

  • Force acting in the opposite direction to an object moving through a fluid (either gas or liquid) (eg. friction, air resistance)
  • A key component of drag forces is that it increases with the speed of the object.

Terminal velocity

  • For a body in free fall, the only force acting is its weight and its acceleration g due to gravity.
  • The drag force increases as the body accelerates
  • Due to Newton’s Second Law, this means the resultant force and therefore acceleration decreases (recall F = ma)
  • When the drag force is equal to the gravitational pull on the body, the body will no longer accelerate and will fall at a constant velocity
    • This the maximum velocity that the object can have and is called the terminal velocity
